Hensodyne

Hensodyne is an American multinational aerospace and defense contractor headquartered in Las Vegas, Nevada.

Hensodyne is most known to the public for creating commercial use airplanes, however, Hensodyne also manufactures rockets, missiles, and other airborne defense applications for commercial and military clientele.

Hensodyne was incorporated on March 16 1954. Originally, Hensodyne was created to develop missiles in the event of a conflict with the Soviet Union. In 1963, while experimenting with chemical compunds to be carried in warheads, a researcher by the name of Conrad Roswell accidentally discovered Cytozine. At the time, Hensodyne did not disclose the existence of or further research Cytozine, though this changed in May 1978 after Roswell's death. Independent research conducted by Roswell discovered the presence of Cytozine embedded in the DNA of a small amount of humans. Roswell called this group "aliysies".

In 1996, Hensodyne conducted the first aliysies conversion on a Delaware man named Ali Slater. Slater was offered $750 USD, but the money was never awarded, because Slater died in the first phase of the procedure. Family of Slater filed a wrongful death lawsuit against Hensodyne, and the case was sesttled out of court for $16.3M USD.
