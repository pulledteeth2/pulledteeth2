Navatrols

Navatrols is an American multinational pharmaceutical and defense manufacturer headquartered in Washington D.C. They are credited with completing the first and only successful Aliysies Conversion in 2007, which they named K93-M52.

Navatrols is most known to the public for developing psychiatric medication, but the company also develops weapons, and contracts soldiers to various military operations around the world.

Navatrols has bases in the following locations:

  • [BASE 1] Washington, D.C., United States
  • [BASE 2] Saskatchewan, Canada
  • [BASE 3] Victoria, Australia
  • [BASE 4] Nebraska, United States
  • [BASE 5] Zacatecas, Mexico
  • [BASE 6] Bolikamsai, Laos
  • [Base 7] Nordland, Norway
  • [BASE 8] Al Qadarif, Sudan
  • [BASE 9] Al Bayda, Yemen

On larger Navatrols bases (ex. BASE 1, BASE 3, and BASE 5), Navatrols personnel are required to live on base grounds. This is, in part, what led to the Navatrols [BASE 3] 2022/06/17 incident.
